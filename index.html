COUNTRY MAKER

Here you will find all of the steps to create a new country in Europa Universalis 4.

SECTION 1: COUNTRY TAG

(1) CREATE A UNIQUE TAG FOR YOUR COUNTRY

A proper tag must be unique. It cannot be shared with any other country in the game. A proper tag must be three letters long. It cannot contain any non-alphabetical characters. The utility below will help you find a unique tag. Simply enter your desired tag and you will be notified if the tag is available or not.

(2) CREATE A UNIQUE NAME FOR YOUR COUNTRY

Once you have a unique tag, it's time to create the name of your country. Like the tag, the country name has to be unique. The utility below will help you find a unique country name. SImply enter your desired name and you will be notified if the name is available or not.

(1) DEFINE THE GRAPHICAL CULTURE

A graphical culture determines what kind of units and advisor portraits your country will have access to. The utility below will show you your options for selecting a graphical culture. Generally, you'll want to match the graphical culture with the cooresponding location of the country.

(2) DEFINE THE COUNTRY COLOR

A country color determines what color the country will appear as in the game. In the utility below, you may choose the red, green, and blue values for the color. Values for these fields must be between 0 and 255. A display of the resulting color appears below.

(3) DEFINE THE HISTORICAL IDEA GROUPS

A country's historical ideas groups define what idea groups will be selected depending on the state date. Non-1444 start dates will use this to fill in idea groups for this country. This will not determine which idea groups the AI country actually picks during play starting from 1444.

(4) DEFINE THE HISTORICAL UNITS

A country's historical units will determine what units the AI version of your country uses when it has access to them. In the utility below, first select the unit group that you'd like your country to have access to and then select which units your country should prefer from that group.

SECTION 3: COUNTRY HISTORY

(1) DEFINE THE GOVERNMENT TYPE

A government provides your country with a unique way of ruling and gives unique bonuses. In the utility below, please select the type of government. Then, governments within that type will become available to choose from.





You can read about government ranks here.

(2) DEFINE THE STARTING MERCANTILISM

Mercantilism determines how much trade power, embargo efficiency, and liberty desires in colonies your nation has. In the ulity below, you can enter a number from 0 to 100.

(3) DEFINE THE TECHNOLOGY GROUP

Your technology group determines your starting institution tech penality and initial technology level. Addionally, you have access to different technologies depending on your technology group.

(4) DEFINE THE STARTING RELIGION

Your starting religion defines the state religion for your country. It does not determine what religion your provinces will have. In the utility below, you can select what religion you'd like your country to follow.




(5) DEFINE THE PRIMARY AND ACCEPTED CULTURES

The primary culture is the culture of your nation or rulers. Accepted cultures are cultures in your nation that have become significant enough to become accepted. In the utiity below, you can define your primary culture and then add accepted cultures. Click here for a list of cultures.


(6) DEFINE THE CAPITAL

Defining the capital sets the capital province for your nation. You can also set it as a fixed capital, in which the AI will never move the capital. Most capitals in the game are unfixed. In the utility below, you can enter which province, defined by a number, is the capital province of your nation. Click here for a list of province IDs.

(7) Add Historical Friends

This is an optional step to define what countries are considered a historical friend of your nation. Historical friends gain a relationship bonus towards one another and are more likely to ally. In the utility below, you can define the names or tags of the nations that should be considered a historical friend for your nation.


(8) Add Historical Rivals

This is an optional step to define what countries are considered a historical rival of your nation. Historical rivals gains a negative relationship towards one another and are more likely to rival. In the utility below, you can define the names or tags of the nations that should be considered a historical rival for your nation.


(9) Add a National Focus

This is an optional step, where you can define what national focus your country should have. In the utility below, pick "Administrative", "Diplomatic", or "Military" for your country to initially focus on.

(10) Determine Army Professionalism

This is an optional step, in which you can define the starting army professionalism of your nation. In the utility below, enter a number from 0 to 100.
